Hercules (the Roman name for the Greek Heracles) is arguably the most famous figure in Greek mythology. His life is a sprawling epic defined by incredible strength, a tragic temper, and a lifelong feud with a goddess.Here is an overview of the legendary life of the son of Zeus.1. Divine Origins and Early LifeHercules was the result of a union between Zeus, the king of the gods, and Alcmene, a mortal woman. Zeus disguised himself as Alcmene's husband to seduce her, leading to the birth of a demigod.Hera’s Wrath: Zeus’s wife, Hera, was famously jealous. She spent Hercules' entire life trying to destroy him. While he was still an infant in his cradle, she sent two serpents to kill him; the baby Hercules strangled them both with his bare hands.The Name: Ironically, "Heracles" means "Glory of Hera," an attempt by his parents to appease the goddess. It didn't work.2. The Great TragedyAs an adult, Hercules became a champion and a family man, marrying Megara and fathering several children. However, Hera struck him with a fit of temporary insanity. In this madness, Hercules killed his wife and children.When he awoke and realized what he had done, he sought purification from the Oracle of Delphi. The Oracle told him that to atone for his sins, he must serve his cousin, King Eurystheus, for twelve years.3. The Twelve LaborsEurystheus, under Hera's influence, set out to give Hercules "impossible" tasks. Death and ApotheosisHercules' death was as dramatic as his life. His second wife, Deianira, was tricked by a centaur named Nessus. Nessus told her that his blood was a love potion; in reality, it was poisoned with the venom of the Hydra.When Deianira feared Hercules was being unfaithful, she smeared the blood on a robe and gave it to him. The robe burned his flesh, causing agonizing pain that he could not escape. Unable to endure it, Hercules built his own funeral pyre and laid upon it.The Transformation: Because of his divine lineage and his unmatched heroism, the mortal half of Hercules perished in the flames, but his divine half ascended to Mount Olympus. There, he finally reconciled with Hera and lived among the gods as the god of strength.
