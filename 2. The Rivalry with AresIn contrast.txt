2. The Rivalry with AresIn contrast to his friendship with Athena, Hercules had a brutal relationship with Ares, the God of War. While Athena represents "strategic" war, Ares represents "bloodlust," and he and Hercules clashed several times.The Battle of Cycnus: Ares’ son, Cycnus, was a bloodthirsty bandit who challenged Hercules. Hercules killed him, which enraged Ares. The god of war descended to fight the hero himself.The Wounding of a God: With Athena shielding Hercules and guiding his spear, Hercules actually managed to wound Ares in the thigh. Ares was forced to flee back to Olympus in shame—one of the few times a mortal (or demigod) bested a major Olympian in direct combat.3. The Apotheosis (Ascension)Athena’s final act of friendship was personal.
