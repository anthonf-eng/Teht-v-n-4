Apollo and Hercules (Heracles) had a fascinating, high-friction relationship. They were half-brothers, both being sons of Zeus, but their personalities often clashed—Apollo represented the refined arts, prophecy, and order, while Hercules represented raw, untamed power.Here is the story of their most famous encounters.1. The Fight for the Delphic TripodThe most famous interaction between the two occurred when Hercules was seeking a cure for a second bout of madness (after he killed his friend Iphitus). He went to the Oracle of Delphi to ask Apollo for guidance.The Snub: The Pythia (Apollo’s priestess) refused to give Hercules an answer because of his blood-guilt.The Theft: Infuriated, Hercules decided he would simply start his own oracle. He grabbed the sacred tripod—the three-legged seat where the priestess sat to deliver prophecies—and tried to walk out with it.The Brawl: Apollo was outraged at the disrespect to his temple and descended from Olympus to stop him. The two brothers engaged in a massive tug-of-war over the tripod.The Resolution: The fight grew so violent that Zeus had to intervene, throwing a thunderbolt between them to break it up. Apollo got his tripod back, but Hercules got his answer: he had to serve as a slave for three years to atone.2. The Ceryneian Hind (The Third Labor)Apollo’s involvement with Hercules often came through his twin sister, Artemis. In his third labor, Hercules had to capture a deer with golden antlers that was sacred to Artemis.After a year of tracking the deer, Hercules finally caught it.On his way back, he was confronted by Apollo and Artemis.Apollo was ready to punish Hercules for the sacrilege, but Hercules used his "silver tongue" (a rare occurrence) to explain that he was under the orders of Eurystheus.Apollo and his sister eventually allowed him to "borrow" the deer, showing a rare moment of divine leniency toward the hero.3. The Shared BowInterestingly, despite their fight over the tripod, Apollo is often credited with giving Hercules one of his most important tools. In some versions of the myth, when the gods were gifting Hercules equipment for his labors:Hermes gave him a sword.Athena gave him a robe.Apollo gave him his famous bow and arrows.This bow would later become crucial when Hercules dipped the arrows in the blood of the Hydra, making them lethal even to the gods.
